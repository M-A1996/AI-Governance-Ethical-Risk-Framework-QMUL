## üß† Overview

This project investigates how organizations can build **responsible AI governance structures** that comply with international ethical and legal standards.
The research integrates **policy analysis, corporate case studies, and ethical framework design** to propose a governance model that ensures **fairness, transparency, accountability, and human oversight** in AI systems.

It is grounded in global frameworks including:

* **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation, 2018)**
* **OECD AI Principles (2019)**
* **UNESCO AI Ethics Recommendation (2021)**

The report evaluates real-world ethical challenges in AI adoption and introduces a **custom Ethical AI Governance Framework** to help organizations implement sustainable and compliant AI systems.

## üéØ Objectives

* Identify key **ethical risks** associated with AI deployment in corporate environments.
* Examine **data ethics failures** and their regulatory implications.
* Assess **organizational governance models** for responsible AI development.
* Design a **practical Ethical AI Governance Framework** aligned with global principles.

## üß© Case Study Highlights

### üá¨üáß British Airways Data Breach (2018)

* One of the largest GDPR enforcement cases in the UK.
* Revealed poor cybersecurity practices and lack of internal accountability.
* Breach of GDPR Articles **25** (Data Protection by Design) and **32** (Security of Processing).
* Resulted in a significant fine from the Information Commissioner‚Äôs Office (ICO).
* Reinforces the need for **ethical data governance and proactive risk management**.

### üåê Infosys Responsible AI Framework

* Demonstrates practical integration of ethics in enterprise AI development.
* Employs **privacy-by-design**, **bias mitigation**, and **explainable AI** across business units.
* Adopts **OECD and UNESCO principles** emphasizing human-centric design.
* Promotes continuous employee training and cross-functional ethical review boards.

## üìà Key Insights

* **Ethics-by-design** must be integrated throughout the AI development cycle.
* **Regulation alone is insufficient** ‚Äî corporate culture and leadership are essential for accountability.
* **Transparency** and **explainability** are fundamental for user trust.
* The **Infosys case** exemplifies how global companies can operationalize responsible AI.
* The **British Airways case** shows that data negligence erodes public confidence and violates regulatory principles.
* A well-structured **Ethical Impact Assessment (EIA)** bridges innovation with compliance.
